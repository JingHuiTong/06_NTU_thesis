Electronic Supplement to
A Database of Shear-Wave Splitting Measurements for the Arabian Plate

by Saleh Qaysi, Kelly H. Liu, and Stephen S. Gao

This electronic supplement contains tables of individual shear-wave splitting measurements, station-averaged splitting parameters, and area-averaged splitting parameters.

Tables

Table S1. The individual shear-wave splitting measurements. The table contains all the 3998 individual measurements presented in the main article. The measurements are plotted in Figure 2 of the main article. The columns are (1) name of the station; (2) phase used for analysis (SKS, SKKS, or PKS); (3) event EQyydddhhmm, in which yy is the year, ddd is the Julian day, hh is the hour, and mm is the minute of the original time (universal time); (4) station latitude; (5) station longitude; (6) fast orientation in degree (clockwise from the north); (7) standard deviation (st. dev.) of fast orientation; (8) splitting time in second; (9) st. dev. of spitting time; (10) back azimuth of the event; (11) modulo-90 degree back azimuth of the event; (12) event latitude; (13) event longitude; (14) focal depth in km; (15) rank for the measurement; (16) latitude of the ray piercing points (200 km depth); and (17) longitude of the ray piercing points (200 km depth).

Table S2. Station-averaged splitting parameters. The table contains all the 182 station-averaged measurements presented in the article. The measurements are plotted in Figure 3 of the main article. The columns are (1) name of the station; (2) station latitude; (3) station longitude; (4) circular mean of the fast orientations observed at the station; (5) circular st. dev. of the fast orientations observed at the station; (6) simple mean of the splitting times observed at the station; (7) simple st. dev. of the splitting times observed at the station; and (8) number of measurements at the station.

Table S3. Area-averaged splitting parameters. The data are plotted in Figure 4 of the main article. The columns in the table are (1) the latitude of the center of the circle; (2) the longitude of the center of the circles; (3) circular mean the fast orientations in the circle; (4) circular st. dev. of the fast orientations in the circle; (5) simple mean of the splitting times; (6) simple st. dev. of the spitting times; (7) the number of individual measurements in the circle.
